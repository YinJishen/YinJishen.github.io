Visualization

The detailed code is on GitHub.

In the first graph, I chose the top 5 countries which have the largest average death rate in the past 20 years. (Sierra Leone, Burkina Faso, Uganda, Equatorial Guinea and Cote d’lvoire)

From this graph, we can see that there are two different trends. For Uganda (Green line), the death rate keeps decreasing. And for the other four countries, the death rate increases in the first 10 years and then decreases.

In the second graph, I simply computed the total number of death for different age and year. We see that the number is quite stable for different year while it shows a significance difference between ages. For children under age 5, the death number is about 10 times as many as other age intervals. It’s important to prevent and cure children from this disease.

In the first graph, I chose the top 5 countries which have the largest average risk rate in the past 20 years. (Sierra Leone, Burkina Faso, Uganda, Equatorial Guinea and Cote d’lvoire). We find this is exactly the same as the first graph. We know that these 5 countries are the hardest hit areas by Malaria.

From this graph, we can see that before 2005, in these countries, half of population are exposed at high risk. And as time elapses, the risk rate is decreasing. What we need to do is keep preventing the spread of this disease and study on the prevention and treatment solution of Malaria.
